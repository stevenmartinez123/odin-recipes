Spanish Rice

Spanish rice is essentially a rice pilaf, but with southwestern flavorings. To make it you brown the raw rice first with onions and garlic, and then cook the rice in chicken broth with added tomatoes.

The browning is essential to the nutty, almost toasty flavor of the rice. And although bouillon can be substituted for the chicken stock, nothing beats homemade chicken stock, whose rich flavor is absorbed by the rice as it cooks.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of medium or long-grain white rice
  • 4 cups of water
  • 1 onion, finely chopped (about 1 cup)
  • 1 garlic clove, minced (about 1 teaspoon)
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil (can use up to 1/4 cup)
  • Pinch of dried oregano
  • 1 Teaspoon of cumin
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 Tablespoon of Chicken Bouillon
  • 3-4 Tablespoons of Tomato Sauce

Steps

  1. Heat up 4 cups of water to a light boil
  2. Pour 2 tablespoons of oil into seperate bowl on medium heat
  3. Chop one onion until diced and place into rice bowl
  4. Chop garlic and place into rice bowl
  5. When rice bowl is warm enough place uncooked rice and cook evenly until rice is golden
  6. In the mean time, season the boiled water with the listed spices and salt
  7. Once rice is cooked evenly place 3-4 tablespoons of tomato sauce into rice and spread until rice is covered evenly
  8. Pour seasons water into rice bowl, lower heat to simmer and place lid over the rice
  9. Place a timer for about 30 minutes to let the rice cook, once time is up do not lift lid for another 10 minutes
  10. Lift lid and stir the rice, Enjoy!!
